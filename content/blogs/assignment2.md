Anonymous or Not? The Ethics of Course Evaluation Surveys


Introduction
Course evaluation surveys are commonly used by colleges and organizations to gather feedback from students and improve educational programs. This blog post analyzes the ethical concerns surrounding a survey that requires students to log in while claiming responses are anonymous.
Understanding the Ethical Problem
The survey is intended to provide actionable insights to management. However, requiring users to log in while promising anonymity raises concerns about privacy, transparency, fairness, accountability, and trust.
Question 1: Ethical Issues
Key issues include lack of transparency, privacy concerns, informed consent challenges, question design bias, and reduced trust in the survey process.
Question 2: Other Ethical Issues
Additional concerns include data security risks, function creep, fear of retaliation, sample bias, and accountability problems.
Question 3: Recommendations
Recommendations include improving transparency, using true anonymization, strengthening consent procedures, enhancing data security, reviewing survey questions, and establishing governance policies.
Question 4: If Management Rejects Recommendations
A data analyst should document concerns, communicate professionally, escalate through appropriate channels if necessary, avoid misleading analysis, and continue following ethical principles.
Conclusion
Ethical data science requires balancing business goals with privacy, fairness, transparency, and accountability. Organizations that prioritize ethics build trust and make better decisions.
